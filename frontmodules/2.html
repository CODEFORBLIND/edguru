Structuring Text with Tags

Sometimes we want to format paragraphs with line breaks.

For example, this song would look much better if Raindrops on roses appeared on one line, Whiskers on kittens on the next, and so on.

Browser

My favorite things

Raindrops on roses.Whiskers on kittens Bright copper kettles Warm woolen mittens

We can separate lines with the help of the line break tag:<br>.
<h1>My favorite things</h1>
<p>Raindrops on roses<_>
Whiskers on kittens<br>
Bright copper kettles<_>
Warm woolen mittens</p>









Output

My favorite things

Raindrops on roses
Whiskers on kittens
Bright copper kettles
Warm woolen mittens

There's an element for giving emphasis to text, it makes it italic. It's called the em element. Add <em> and </em>. <h1>My favorite things<h1>
<p> Raindrops on __ roses __ <br>
Whiskers on kittens<br>
Bright copper kettles<br>
Warm woolen mittens </p>









Output

My favorite things

Raindrops on roses
Whiskers on kittens
Bright copper kettles
Warm woolen mittens

To define texts as important, we use the <strong> and </strong> tags. It makes text bold. <h1>My favorite things</h1>
<p> Raindrops on <em>roses </em>
Whiskers on _ kittens _ </p>









Output

My favorite things

Raindrops on roses
Whiskers on kittens
